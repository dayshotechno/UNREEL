Dramaturgy and Narrative Architecture for ReelsWhen the tool is tasked with compiling fully finished Reels (using the presets), it must adhere to algorithmic best practices for social media.2.1 The Hook Phase (Seconds 0 to 3)The script must isolate the most engaging visual or spoken moment for the first three seconds. For spoken content, prioritize "Anti-Advice" hooks (debunking a myth) over generic introductions. Visually, this requires an immediate, hard cut to the subject. Fade-ins or black frames at the beginning must be strictly disabled in the render pipeline.2.2 Musical SynchronizationIn EDM, the audio dictates the visual pacing. The system must utilize librosa.beat.beat\_track to find downbeats and transient peaks. The frequency of visual cuts should exponentially increase during a musical build-up and rest on a strong, uncut shot during the main drop.2.3 Seamless Looping MechanicsTo force continuous replays, the generated video must not fade to black. The editing matrix must match the end frame's motion vector and audio phase perfectly with the first frame. Instagram Reels do not have a built-in loop toggle, so the loop must be natively encoded into the file without any audio artifact or "bump".3. Core Pipeline Features (Implementation Directives for the Agent)The following four technical features are mandatory for the Python tool to achieve production-grade output.3.1 AI Auto-Framing and Subject Tracking (9:16 Crop)Source material is shot in 16:9, but outputs must be 9:16. A static center crop is unacceptable as the DJ moves across the booth.Implementation: Use PySceneDetect to slice the video into scenes first (to prevent tracking errors across hard cuts).Use Ultralytics YOLOv11 for instance segmentation to identify the DJ, combined with ByteTrack (via the lap library) to maintain the track ID across frames.Integrate MediaPipe to detect facial and pose landmarks for framing cues. Apply motion damping and virtual camera smoothing so the vertical crop follows the DJ naturally.3.2 Automated Audio Synchronization (Cross-Correlation)The script must automatically sync the low-quality camera scratch audio with the high-quality master Line-In recording.Implementation: Extract PCM audio samples from both files using FFmpeg.Convert the arrays to the frequency domain via FFT and use scipy.signal.correlate to find the exact millisecond offset (lag) where the arrays match.Shift the Line-In track accordingly and stitch it with \~10% of the original camera audio to retain live crowd noise.3.3 Consistent Color Grading (3D LUTs)The output must maintain a consistent, cinematic brand identity regardless of venue lighting.Implementation: Use a 3D .cube LUT. The agent should configure FFmpeg to apply the LUT using the haldclut filter (by overlaying a modified PNG hald image) or the lut3d video filter to globally normalize skin tones and harsh club LEDs.3.4 Transcription and Hardcoded SubtitlesSpoken Vlogs must have dynamic subtitles, as mobile users watch on mute by default.Implementation: Extract the audio and run it through OpenAI Whisper to generate highly accurate, word-level timestamps.Use MoviePy (TextClip and SubtitlesClip) to programmatically burn (hardcode) the subtitles into the lower third of the 9:16 frame, ensuring they do not overlap with native TikTok/Instagram UI elements.4.
